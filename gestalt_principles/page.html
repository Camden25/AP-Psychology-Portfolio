Gestalt Principles Picture Book

Gestalt psychology explains how humans naturally organize visual elements into groups or unified wholes. Below, you will find examples and explanations of key Gestalt principles.

Closure

Definition: Closure refers to the mind's ability to fill in gaps to perceive a complete image.

Explanation: In this image, partial outlines of a shape are presented. Even though parts of the shape are missing, the brain perceives it as a whole due to the principle of closure.

Figure and Ground

Definition: Figure and ground refer to the visual separation of objects (figure) from their background (ground).

Explanation: This image shows how the vase (figure) and the background (ground) alternate depending on what you focus on. This demonstrates how the brain separates visual elements into distinct layers.

Proximity

Definition: Proximity refers to the grouping of objects that are physically close to each other.

Explanation: In this image, individual dots are arranged closer to one another in certain areas. The brain groups them into clusters, illustrating the principle of proximity.

Similarity

Definition: Similarity refers to the grouping of objects that are visually similar in shape, size, color, or other attributes.

Explanation: This image shows circles and squares. The brain groups the shapes by their similarity, organizing circles into one group and squares into another.
