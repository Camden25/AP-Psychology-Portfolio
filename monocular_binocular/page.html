Photo Presentation: Monocular/Binocular Cues

Below is a collection of original photos and explanations that demonstrate key terms related to Monocular and Binocular Cues, as well as Perceptual Constancy and Change Blindness.

Monocular Cues

Relative Size

My shoe is closer to the camera than rohail so it looks like I am crushing him with my show even though my shoe is much smaller than him. We know the shoe is closer because it is larger than it should be.

Interposition

One of my dogs covers part of my other dog so we know which dog is in front.

Texture Gradient

My dog's fur looks less textured from farther away so you can tell how far away my dogs is.

Relative Clarity

We know that the chair is very close to the camera because it is blurry. It is not at the distance we are focusing on so it is not clear.

Linear Perspective

The road converges to a point the farther away it is so we can tell distance of objects based off of that.

Size Constancy

Shape Constancy

Retinal Disparity

Retinal disparity refers to the slight difference in the images perceived by each eye due to their horizontal separation. This difference helps our brain perceive depth.

Photo from the left perspective

Photo from the right perspective

In the two photos, you can see the slight differences in perspective, which simulate how each eye views the world. Our brain uses these differences to calculate depth.
