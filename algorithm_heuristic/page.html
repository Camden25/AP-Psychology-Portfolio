Algorithm vs. Heuristic Problem-Solving Challenge

This activity explores the differences between algorithmic and heuristic approaches to problem-solving. Each method is applied to a specific puzzle, and their efficiency and accuracy are compared.

Scenario 1: Jigsaw Puzzle

Algorithm Approach: Sorting pieces by color and shape, starting with edges.

Heuristic Approach: Focusing on distinctive pieces or assembling recognizable sections.

Discussion: The heuristic and algorithmic approaches took around the same amount of time. The heuristic approach started much faster but slowed down due to the pieces not being sorted.

Scenario 2: Word Search Puzzle

Algorithm Approach: Systematically scanning rows and columns.

Heuristic Approach: Looking for familiar patterns or starting with the longest words.

Discussion: Analyze the results of the algorithmic and heuristic approaches. Which method proved faster or more accurate? Discuss any potential trade-offs and describe how each approach might be applied in real-world problem-solving scenarios.
